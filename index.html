Shirts

If we had a mole of t-shirts, we'd have quite a lot of shirts. But what exactly is the scale of 6.02 × 10²³ tshirts?

First, lets see how big a shirt really is.


We piled 32 t-shirts on top of each other to get the thickness of a t-shirt. Using a ruler, the pile was 4 cm tall.
Some quick converstions give us our results. \[ \frac{4 \text{ cm}}{32 \text{ t-shirts}} = \frac{1}{8} \text{ cm per t-shirt.} \] More test information.
